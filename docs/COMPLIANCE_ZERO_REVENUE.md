# Zero-Revenue Compliance: Brazilian Legal Requirements

This document serves as the canonical reference for the regulatory rules encoded in RCal's zero-revenue advisory system. It outlines the obligations for a **solopreneur (sole owner-administrator, no employees)** under **Simples Nacional, Anexo III**, specifically in **Florianópolis, SC**.

## 1. Pró-labore Obligations (Owner's Salary)

- **General Rule:** Brazilian legislation establishes that the *sócio administrador* (owner-administrator) who works in the company is a mandatory INSS contributor.
- **Zero-Revenue Scenario:** If the company has **zero revenue and is genuinely inactive** in a given month, there is NO legal obligation to withdraw a Pró-labore.
- **Risk of Non-Withdrawal:** If the company has *any* operational activity (even without formal revenue) and no Pró-labore is withdrawn, the Receita Federal may question it — particularly if profits are distributed later without prior INSS contribution.
- **RCal's Approach:** RCal advises the user that Pró-labore is optional during zero-revenue months ("inactive" status), but clearly warns that choosing not to withdraw a salary means losing INSS social security coverage for that specific month.

## 2. DAS (Simples Nacional Tax)

- **General Rule:** The Documento de Arrecadação do Simples Nacional (DAS) is calculating by applying an effective tax rate to the gross monthly revenue.
- **Zero-Revenue Scenario:** Because the tax is a percentage of revenue, **DAS is strictly R$ 0,00** when revenue is zero.
- **Reporting Obligation:** Crucially, the company **must still file** the monthly PGDAS-D declaration (informing R$ 0,00 revenue). Failure to file this "zero-revenue" declaration generates automatic fines.

## 3. INSS Contributions

- **Employee Portion (11%):** Only due if a Pró-labore is actually withdrawn. No Pró-labore = No 11% INSS tax.
- **Patronal Portion (20%):** For companies under Anexo III, the employer's INSS contribution (CPP) is **already included** within the DAS tax rate. Since DAS is zero when revenue is zero, there is no separate patronal INSS payment due. Companies in Anexo IV would have to pay this separately, but RCal is strictly designed for Anexo III.

## 4. FGTS

- **General Rule:** FGTS (Fundo de Garantia do Tempo de Serviço) applies specifically to employees under the CLT regime.
- **Solopreneur Scenario:** The owner-administrator has a *societária* (corporate) relationship with the company, rather than an employment relationship. Therefore, **FGTS is NOT mandatory** for the business owner. While a company *may* choose to voluntarily deposit FGTS for a director, this is entirely optional and uncommon for solopreneurs.

## 5. State & Municipal Obligations (SC / Florianópolis)

- **ISS (Municipal Services Tax):** For service exporters, ISS is already exempted (this is factored into RCal's Anexo III DAS calculation). With zero revenue, ISS is naturally zero.
- **ICMS (State Tax):** Not applicable, as the company provides services rather than circulating goods.
- **TFF (Taxa de Fiscalização de Funcionamento):** The municipality of Florianópolis charges a **fixed annual licensing fee** to all active CNPJs, regardless of whether the company generated revenue during the year. RCal surfaces this as a compliance reminder, as it represents a mandatory operational cost outside the standard monthly taxes.

## 6. Business Suspension & Closure

When facing expected long-term zero revenue, solopreneurs have specific paths to manage their CNPJ:

1. **Keep Active:** CNPJ remains active. Must continue filing the monthly PGDAS-D (even if zero) and the annual DEFIS, as well as handling eSocial "sem movimento" declarations.
2. **Temporary Suspension:** A formal communication to the Receita Federal (via the Redesim portal) to temporarily halt activities. The CNPJ status changes to "Suspensa". Note that this *does not* relieve the company of all declarative obligations.
3. **Formal Closure (Baixa):** The permanent closure of the CNPJ. After final declarations are submitted, all ongoing obligations and fixed costs (like the TFF) cease.
